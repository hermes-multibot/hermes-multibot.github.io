Multi-Robot Operations in Extreme Environments

In recent decades, deploying robots in extreme environments has become quintessential, given the significant risks and challenges these environments pose to human safety or inaccessibility. Whether it is the autonomous exploration and mapping of mining tunnels, or monitoring cryospheric processes on Earth, or sampling regolith at the lunar south pole - robots have the ability to function and adapt to these otherwise inaccessible areas, performing complex tasks without human presence or fixed infrastructure.

Utilizing heterogeneity—whether in behavior, physical design, or both— multi-robot systems can significantly improve spatio-temporal data efficiency, enhance measurement accuracy, avoid single point of failures and boost mission resilience and return on investment. However, navigating the many obstacles in these extreme and inaccessible environments present various challenges—such as irregular lighting, featureless and unstructured terrain, uneven surfaces, and GNSS-denied localization, timing and navigation. In this 3rd edition of the HERMES (Heterogeneous Multi-Robot Cooperation for Exploration & Science in Extreme Environments) workshop, we will bring scientists and emerging researchers to address and advance the state of the art on autonomous, remote and tele-operations of cooperative heterogeneous robots in extreme environments.

Objectives

Our goal is to explore and identify new solutions to enhance the spatial and situational awareness of multi-robot teams operating within and across extreme on- and off-Earth environments. Three primary objectives have been identified for this year's edition of the workshop:

  • to identify critical challenges for tele-, remote and autonomous operations of a heterogeneous team of multi-robotic systems in science, technological and commercial missions across extreme terrestrial and non-terrestrial environments

  • to understand how the cooperative, autonomous and heterogeneous nature of multi-robotic systems can improve the robustness, resilience and reliability of diverse mission operations

  • to explore key knowledge areas where further investigation and research is required, and potential opportunity for collaboration among participants on complementary expertise

Topics Covered

  • Science and technological requirements for multi-robot operations.
  • Recent advancements in communication-constrained and/or GNSS-denied multi-robot operations including novel approaches in Positioning, Navigation, Tracking, Mapping, Data Sharing, and Communications.
  • Traditional and learning-based approaches for Inference, Control, Coordination and Decision making in multi-agent robotics.
  • Efficient and adaptable planning and coordination of heterogeneous multi-robotic missions, associated tasks, and resources.
  • Challenges in reliable and robust teleoperation and the development of human-machine interfaces including augmented, virtual, and extended reality.
  • Technological challenges for extreme terrain mobility and innovative locomotion systems.
  • Insights gained from developing novel simulators, experiments, datasets, and the results obtained from analogue and real scientific missions and field tests.
  • Investigate commercial opportunities in multi-robot cooperation for Earth and space.
  • Scalability challenges in multi-robot operations.
