Objectives

The workshop is aimed at showcasing the achievements, capabilities, and approaches of relevant institutions and laboratories within the frame of multi-agent systems in extreme operational environments. Three primary goals have been identified:

  • to identify high priority scientific and exploratory tasks, on- and off-Earth, where deploying teams of robots can provide a substantial benefit

  • to understand how heterogeneous teams can effectively cooperate to map, characterize, reason, and act in extreme, dynamic, and unstructured environments

  • to define key focus areas for education, research, and technology development in the upcoming years, within the crossroad of robotics, exploration, and science in extreme environments

Topics Covered

  • Status of and challenges in performing science and exploration in extreme environments, including but not limited to oceans, glaciers, arctic regions, volcanoes, hot-springs, caves, underground tunnels, planetary surfaces, and asteroids.
  • Definition of immediate needs and scientific priorities for the deployment of heterogeneous multi-robot teams for Earth and Space.
  • Recent findings and developments in the fields of multi-agent robot localization, mapping, coordination, communication, task planning, sampling, and cooperative sensing, learning and decision making.
  • Presentation of novel mobile robotic concepts and prototypes for the exploration of unconstructed, dynamic environments.
  • Dissemination of lessons learned from the development and field testing of heterogeneous multi-robot teams.
  • Exploration of commercial ventures in the field of multi-robot cooperation for Earth and Space.
